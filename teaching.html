Teaching overview

My teaching mainly takes place in Language Sciences (SCL) and Cognitive Sciences (SCO), at undergraduate and graduate levels. Courses combine theoretical perspectives on meaning, discourse and interaction with corpus-based and computational approaches.

Supervision

I supervise Master’s and PhD projects at the interface of linguistics, conversation analysis, corpus studies, and computational approaches to language, with a strong interest in spontaneous speech and interaction.

Advice for prospective students

If you are considering Master’s or PhD supervision with me, please take the following points into account:

  • You should have a genuine interest in computational and/or quantitative approaches to language. A good way to assess this is to browse my recent publications (see the research page).
  • On the practical side, I work with git, Python, and LaTeX. I do not use Microsoft tools for writing papers or slides. I will not teach how to use these tools from scratch: students are expected to learn them autonomously.
