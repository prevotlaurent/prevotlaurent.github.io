I like to walk in the hills and the mountains. I like to watch birds. I am still debating whether taking pictures is a way to get closer to life, or a way to escape the rest.

I also like to show—and to remind—that Taiwan cannot be reduced to its modern, bustling, cosmopolitan, tolerant big cities. Taiwan hosts more than a dozen Austronesian languages and associated people groups, hundreds of 3000m peaks, bears hidden in deep forested hills, many endemic species, and many islands, each with its own varieties.
